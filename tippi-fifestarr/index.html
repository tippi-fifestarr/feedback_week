Game Blog

Puddles' Crazy Drift 2 Finish

your borser doesnt support html5 images elements?

After immersing in Chainlink Lab's SmartCon Summit #1, my brains were fully filled with blocks. To celebrate 3 days of productive learning, networking, and adding value best I could, I loaded up Puddle's MacRally with the goal in mind.

A game made by one of the greatest musicians of our generation, there is no sound effects or music. Really gives us something to look forward as Tom develops it further.

It's devilishly tough, which is a quality rarely found in games today. For a while, I thought the timing of the jumps and drifting was synced to some BPM.

Persistence got me to one of the several finish lines, and very close to others (intrensically motivating to complete the level). As a game player, it would be rewarding to have my high score saved and to see a victory screen.

One missing skill on my first few days of play was how to control the car in air to catch the tight turns (especially after that rail on the left route).

I do not doubt that Tom can make this game bigger and better all by himself, but I know the power of teamwork is one key that can unlock the maximum potential of his genius

For an example of how to weave story and branding into a simple Unity game, here is a link to Tennyson King's Life on Shore gamewhich unlocks a music video when you complete the single level.
